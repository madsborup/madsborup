### Case

Montem has been developing city probes and would like to offer IoT-based services in the future. In order to design meaningful interactions with the probes, Montem would like to follow a human-centric approach to prevent IoT systems from being purely technology driven. This case is part of a larger project aiming for transparency and sustainability, allowing citizens to interpret their everyday environment and react to changes in air quality - information and understanding is a key parameter in changing behaviours.

### Method

We saw the project as an opportunity to try a new method, mainly the "Sprint" method by Jake Knapp. 

![Hello Monti App](/media/projects/hello-monti/sprint.png)

### Identifying opportunities

We did extensive user research to understand how users use their city, primarily while commuting and their context around that. Through this, we identified opportunities for expanding specific moments and activities in their lives in relation to Montems business. This research also informed our decision to design a conversational UI, as this allowed a back-and-forth communication with the user and the system and for the users to understand opportunities for their current context based on Montems data.

![Hello Monti App](/media/projects/hello-monti/analysis.png)

### Solution

We built a functional app that allowed users to talk to Monti, the collective mind of all Montem's probes. By giving information such as current mental state, users got suggestions from Monti about locations in the city to relax, based on real-time data (like noise, pollution, sunlight) from Montem's probes.
